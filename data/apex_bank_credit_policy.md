## Section 1 — Eligibility Criteria

### 1.1 Minimum Credit Score Requirements

| Loan Type | Minimum Credit Score | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| Home Loan | 700 | CIBIL / Experian |
| Personal Loan | 720 | CIBIL only |
| Vehicle Loan | 680 | Any bureau |
| Business Loan | 700 | Business + personal score averaged |
| Loan Against Property (LAP) | 660 | Lower due to collateral |

Applications below these thresholds are automatically declined at the pre-screening stage.
Exceptions up to 20 points below threshold require Branch Manager approval (Form CR-07).

### 1.2 Age Criteria

- Minimum age at application: 23 years
- Maximum age at loan maturity: 65 years (salaried), 70 years (self-employed, professionals)
- Co-applicant can extend maturity age by a maximum of 5 years

### 1.3 Employment and Income Criteria

**Salaried Applicants:**
- Minimum employment continuity: 2 years total, 6 months current employer
- Minimum net monthly income: ₹25,000 (Tier 1 cities), ₹18,000 (Tier 2/3)
- Acceptable employers: Government, PSU, listed companies, MNC, or any company on the approved employer list (AEL)

**Self-Employed / Business Owners:**
- Minimum business vintage: 3 years
- ITR filing: mandatory for last 2 years
- Net annual profit (after tax): minimum ₹3,00,000

## Section 2 — Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Policy

### 2.1 DTI Calculation Method

DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Obligations ÷ Gross Monthly Income) × 100

Obligations included: all existing EMIs, proposed new EMI, credit card minimum payment (5% of outstanding balance), and rental obligations if any.

### 2.2 DTI Limits by Loan Type

| Loan Type | Maximum DTI | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| Home Loan | 45% | Up to 50% with compensating factors |
| Personal Loan | 40% | Strict — no exceptions without senior approval |
| Vehicle Loan | 45% | |
| Business Loan | 50% | Business cash flow considered separately |
| LAP | 55% | Rental income can offset up to 70% of EMI |

### 2.3 Compensating Factors for DTI Override

The following may allow a DTI up to 5 percentage points above the standard limit:
1. Credit score ≥ 780 (excellent credit history)
2. LTV ≤ 60% (significant equity)
3. Liquid assets ≥ 6 months of total EMI obligations
4. Co-applicant income with DTI bringing combined ratio within limit

All DTI overrides require Credit Manager approval and documentation in the credit note.

## Section 3 — Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

### 3.1 Standard LTV Limits

| Loan Amount | Maximum LTV (Home Loan) |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Up to ₹30 lakhs | 90% |
| ₹30 lakhs to ₹75 lakhs | 80% |
| Above ₹75 lakhs | 75% |

### 3.2 LTV for Other Secured Products

- Vehicle Loan: 85% of on-road price (new vehicles), 75% of valuation (used vehicles)
- LAP: 60% of market value (residential), 50% (commercial property)

### 3.3 Valuation Requirements

Properties above ₹50 lakhs must have an independent valuation from a bank-empanelled valuer. Valuation report must be dated within 90 days of application.

## Section 4 — Documentation Requirements

### 4.1 Universal Documents (All Loan Types)

- Identity proof: Aadhaar card (mandatory) + one of PAN card, Passport, Voter ID
- Address proof: Aadhaar card (if current address matches) or utility bill (< 3 months)
- PAN card: mandatory for all applicants and co-applicants
- Passport-size photographs: 2 (applicant), 2 (co-applicant if any)

### 4.2 Income Documents — Salaried

- Last 3 months salary slips
- Last 6 months bank statement (salary account)
- Latest Form 16 (or ITR acknowledgement)
- Offer letter / appointment letter (if < 1 year with current employer)

### 4.3 Income Documents — Self-Employed

- Last 2 years ITR with computation and acknowledgement
- Last 2 years audited financial statements (P&L, balance sheet)
- Last 12 months bank statement (primary business account)
- GST registration certificate (if applicable)
- Business proof: trade licence / shops & establishment certificate

### 4.4 Property Documents (Home Loan / LAP)

- Title deed chain for minimum 13 years
- Approved building plan (municipality)
- Encumbrance certificate (EC) — last 13 years
- Sale agreement / MOU
- NOC from builder (under-construction properties)

## Section 5 — Processing Timelines

### 5.1 Standard TAT (Turnaround Time)

| Stage | Salaried | Self-Employed |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Pre-screening decision | Same day | 1 business day |
| Document verification | 2 business days | 3 business days |
| Credit assessment | 1 business day | 2 business days |
| Property valuation (if applicable) | 5-7 business days | 5-7 business days |
| Sanction letter issuance | 1 business day post-assessment | 1 business day |
| Disbursement (after all docs received) | 3-5 business days | 5-7 business days |

### 5.2 Priority Processing

Applications qualify for priority TAT (50% faster) when:
- Applicant is an Apex Bank Salary Account holder for ≥ 1 year
- Pre-approved offer is active on the CRM (indicated by green "Pre-Approved" flag)
- Loan amount ≤ ₹10 lakhs (express personal loan track)

## Section 6 — Interest Rates and Fees

### 6.1 Base Interest Rate Structure

All retail loan interest rates are linked to the Apex Bank Base Rate (ABBR), reviewed quarterly.
Current ABBR: 8.50% per annum (effective January 2026)

| Product | Rate | Spread over ABBR |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| Home Loan (salaried, CIBIL ≥ 750) | 8.75% – 9.25% p.a. | +0.25% to +0.75% |
| Home Loan (self-employed) | 9.00% – 9.75% p.a. | +0.50% to +1.25% |
| Personal Loan | 11.50% – 14.00% p.a. | Fixed spread, risk-based |
| Vehicle Loan | 9.00% – 11.00% p.a. | +0.50% to +2.50% |
| Business Loan | 12.00% – 15.00% p.a. | +3.50% to +6.50% |
| LAP | 10.00% – 12.00% p.a. | +1.50% to +3.50% |

### 6.2 Fees and Charges

- Processing fee: 0.50% of loan amount (minimum ₹2,000, maximum ₹25,000)
- Prepayment penalty: NIL (floating rate loans, per RBI directive)
- Late payment fee: 2% per month on overdue EMI amount
- CERSAI charge: ₹50 (up to ₹5 lakhs), ₹100 (above ₹5 lakhs)
- Legal and valuation charges: at actuals (non-refundable)

## Section 7 — Decline and Cancellation

### 7.1 Mandatory Decline Conditions

Applications must be declined (no exceptions) when:
- Applicant is on the RBI defaulter list
- Any active DPD (days past due) > 90 on any existing loan in last 24 months
- Undisclosed liabilities found during verification that breach DTI limits
- Document forgery detected — refer to Fraud Risk team (Form FR-01) immediately

### 7.2 Decline Communication

- Written decline notice must be issued within 2 business days of decision
- Reason must be stated in plain language (not internal codes)
- Applicants may reapply after 90 days from the decline date
- Decline reason must not reference protected characteristics (RBI Fair Practice Code)
